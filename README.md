# McKee Business & Technology Building  
## IT Operations & Asset Management

This repository serves as a centralized record of technical operations, instructional technology support, and asset management activities conducted under the **Technical Manager & Instructional Technology Liaison** role for the **McKee Business & Technology Building** at Oakwood University.

Its purpose is to document operational work performed, systems assessed, and processes implemented to improve technology reliability, accountability, and instructional effectiveness across the building.

---

## Role Scope and Responsibilities

Work documented in this repository aligns with the following operational areas:

- Instructional technology and laboratory support
- Desktop and equipment asset inventory management
- Cable management remediation and system validation
- Technical operations for academic and institutional events
- Process documentation and operational standardization
- Evaluation of instructional technology models and infrastructure planning

---

## Asset and Laboratory Management

This section contains documentation related to equipment accountability, system readiness, and maintenance oversight, including:

- Desktop asset inventory records
- Cable management assessments
- System functionality validation logs
- Asset identification and naming conventions
- Equipment readiness and usage standards

These artifacts support informed decision-making regarding maintenance, remediation, and future technology investments.

---

## Research and Evaluation

This repository includes research and assessment materials used to evaluate instructional technology models, including:

- Desktop viability assessments
- Comparative analysis of desktop, laptop, and BYOD environments
- Operational and maintenance cost considerations
- Pilot recommendations for lab modernization

All evaluations are based on verified inventory data and direct observation.

---

## Instructional Technology Support

Documentation in this area reflects support provided for instructional environments, including:

- Classroom and laboratory technology observations
- Faculty support workflows and issue tracking
- Identified instructional technology gaps
- Coordination and liaison documentation with campus IT services

---

## Events and Technical Operations

Records related to technical support for academic and institutional events, including:

- Equipment setup and teardown procedures
- Pre-event system validation checklists
- Post-event technical debriefs and observations
- Continuous improvement recommendations

---

## Documentation and Process Standards

This repository contains standardized documentation intended to ensure consistency and sustainability, including:

- Standard operating procedures (SOPs)
- Validation and inspection checklists
- Issue categorization and prioritization frameworks
- Process documentation supporting role continuity and scalability

## Objectives and Outcomes

The documented work supports the following objectives:

- Improve instructional technology reliability and consistency
- Establish formal asset management practices
- Enable data-informed infrastructure decisions
- Enhance faculty and student technology experiences
- Create repeatable and scalable operational systems
